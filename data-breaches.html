Data Breaches

üîì What is a Data Breach?

A data breach occurs when unauthorized individuals gain access to confidential or sensitive information. This can include personal data like names, addresses, social security numbers, medical records, financial data, and business secrets.

Data breaches often happen through hacking, malware, phishing attacks, or simple human error. Once stolen, this data may be sold on dark web marketplaces or used for identity theft, fraud, or corporate espionage.

üíª How Data Breaches Happen

  • Hackers exploit vulnerabilities in systems or software.
  • Employees accidentally send sensitive information to the wrong recipients.
  • Phishing emails trick staff into giving up credentials.
  • Lost or stolen devices contain unencrypted data.
  • Third-party vendors have weak security practices, leading to breaches.

No organization, big or small, is completely immune from the risk of a data breach.

üõ° How to Stay Safe from Data Breaches

  • Use unique, strong passwords for different accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • Monitor credit reports and bank accounts for suspicious activity.
  • Be cautious with sharing personal data online.
  • Stay updated on breaches to know if your data was exposed.
