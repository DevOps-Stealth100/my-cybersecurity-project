Hacking & Unauthorized Access

üíª What is Hacking?

Hacking is when cybercriminals break into computers, phones, or networks without permission. They steal personal data, drain bank accounts, leak secrets, hold files for ransom, or crash websites. For victims, the damage can be financial loss, identity theft, blackmail, or chaos in businesses and governments.

Hackers exploit weaknesses in technology or trick people into handing over passwords and sensitive information. No one is too small to be a target.

üîß How Hackers Break In

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages that fool you into revealing passwords or financial details.
  • Malware: Software that spies on you, steals data, or locks your files until you pay a ransom.
  • Brute Force Attacks: Guessing millions of password combinations until they get in.
  • Man-in-the-Middle: Intercepting data between you and a website, like reading your banking info on public Wi-Fi.
  • Exploiting Software Bugs: Attacking flaws in apps or websites that haven‚Äôt been updated or patched.

Hackers often combine these methods for bigger attacks that are hard to trace.

  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • Keep your devices and apps updated with the latest security patches.
  • Be skeptical of emails, texts, or calls asking for personal information.
  • Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking unless you‚Äôre using a VPN.

Hackers thrive on people letting their guard down. Staying alert is your best defense.
