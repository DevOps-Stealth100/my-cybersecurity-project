Hacking

Hackers break into people's devices through phishing, sending fake emails or links that give them access to your details, allowing them to steal data.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks trick people into revealing personal info by pretending to be trusted organizations via email, SMS, or calls.

Identity Theft & Government Fraud

Criminals steal personal details like national IDs, bank info, or social security numbers to commit fraud, apply for loans, or claim government benefits.

Business Email Compromise (BEC) Frauds

Hackers break into business email accounts and trick staff into sending large sums of money to fake accounts.

Ransomware

Ransomware locks files or entire systems and demands payment to unlock them, targeting businesses, hospitals, schools, and governments.

Crypto Draining Scams

Scammers trick users into connecting crypto wallets to fake apps or sites, then drain all funds instantly. Fast, silent‚Äîand devastating.

Crypto & Investment Scams

Scammers create fake investment platforms or crypto coins, promising huge returns and disappearing with people's money.

Romance Scams

Scammers pretend to be in love online to gain trust, then ask for money for emergencies, travel, or fake investments.

Social Engineering

Criminals use psychology to manipulate people into revealing secrets, transferring funds, or installing malware.

Online Shopping Scams

Fake stores advertise cheap products online, take your money, but never deliver the goods.

Malware & Spyware

Malicious software steals data, records keystrokes, or hijacks devices to spy on victims or steal money.

ATM & Card Cloning

Criminals use hidden devices on ATMs to copy your card data and steal money from your account.

Data Breaches

Hackers break into databases and steal millions of user records, often selling them on the dark web.

Fake Job Recruitment Scams

Scammers pretend to offer jobs, collect personal info, or demand payments for fake processing fees.

Sextortion & Online Extortion

Criminals threaten to release private photos or videos unless victims pay money.

Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Attacks

Hackers flood a website with traffic to shut it down, often for ransom or political messages.
