üîç Common Red Flags of Online Scams

  • Emails or texts asking for personal or banking info
  • Messages with spelling mistakes or urgent threats
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers (free money, gifts, prizes)
  • Unknown links or attachments in emails and DMs
  • Fake social media accounts impersonating real brands

üé≠ How Cybercriminals Trick You

Scammers use psychological tricks like fear, urgency, curiosity, or fake trust. They might pretend to be your bank, a government agency, or even someone you know. Always pause, think, and verify before responding to any request.

üõ° How to Stay Protected Online

  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Update your software and antivirus regularly
  • Never click suspicious links or open strange attachments
  • Check for HTTPS and spelling in web addresses
  • Don't share sensitive info via text, DMs, or email
